Current Limitations in Analysis:

  • Modalities Missing: The analysis assumed availability of all key MRI sequences.   • Normative Data Gaps: While we referenced general norms, the most accurate assessment uses large, demographically-matched normative databases. Our Z-scores are based on available references (which are pretty robustcortechs.ai, but mostly from general population data). Individual variability in brain structure is significant. So a limitation is potential slight misestimation of “percentile” if your demographics differ from the norm samples.   • Segmentation/Tract Errors: Automated MRI analysis isn’t perfect. Minor segmentation errors (e.g., hippocampal subfield mix-ups or slightly off white matter parcellations) could bias some volume/FA numbers. We did QC and didn’t see major issues, but subtle ones could exist. For example, if part of the amygdala got included with hippocampus, that could inflate volume a bit. We assume errors are consistent over time (so change detection is okay). If a particular value seemed odd (none did glaringly), we’d validate manually. As a next step, we could manually inspect or adjust any region that seems off (none critical so far).

  • Causal Interpretation: We must be careful – we observed strong areas and we assume training those areas leads to performance gains. While generally true (e.g., bigger hippocampus correlates with better memorypmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov), it’s not a guarantee of easy improvement. And a weaker area being trained doesn’t always yield a structural change – sometimes function improves without volume change, etc. Also, increases in volume or FA with training are still a developing science; not everyone shows MRI-detectable changes even if performance improves. We’ve based suggestions on current evidence, but individual responses vary.

  • Generalizability: Our recommendations draw from studies on various populations (aging, training studies, etc.). You as an individual might respond differently. For instance, dual n-back might yield big gains in one person, modest in another.   • Partial Volume Effects: Some measures like cortical thickness can be affected by head positioning or slight differences in scanner calibration even though we tried to control for it. For next steps, ensuring identical scan protocols and maybe even using phantom-based calibration at each session will help firm up any detected change.
